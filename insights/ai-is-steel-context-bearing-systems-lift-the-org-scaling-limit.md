Zhao's structural metaphor: before steel, 19th-century buildings hit a ceiling of "six or seven floors" because iron was "strong but brittle and heavy" and collapsed under its own weight. Steel — "strong yet malleable" — let frames go lighter and buildings rise dozens of stories. He argues "AI is steel for organizations": it "maintain[s] context across workflows and surface[s] decisions when needed without the noise," so "human communication no longer has to be the load-bearing wall." Concretely, "the weekly two-hour alignment meeting becomes a five-minute async review," and orgs "can scale, truly scale, without the degradation we've accepted as inevitable."

The load-bearing claim is the precise inverse of [[context-centralization-is-why-coding-ai-works]]: coding scaled because git already centralizes context; knowledge orgs degrade at scale because their context lives in meetings and human memory. Make software carry that context and the scaling limit lifts. This is the mechanism beneath [[dissolved-constraints-shift-bottleneck-to-judgment]] at the firm level — communication overhead was a binding constraint, and removing it shifts the bottleneck upward to judgment and [[organizational-shape-is-the-emerging-moat-in-ai]]. It also depends on durable [[agent-memory-preserves-institutional-knowledge]], since context can only bear load if it persists.

Gupta names the new failure mode: "In SaaS, you were usually killed by another company... There was one exception: a company built on top of someone else's platform could be wiped out by a pricing or terms change, no competitor involved. AI makes that exception the default." The threat is not a faster team — it is **layer migration**: "Work moves when any variable changes enough: capability, cost, latency, deployment, regulation, trust. It can move into the model, an open-weight alternative, the customer's data platform, an agent runtime, or the device itself." Demand compresses the timeline too — "buyers... know what lock-in costs now, so the arbitrage in that lag is closing."

This is the threat-model consequence of [[ai-strategy-is-a-self-rewriting-equation]]: when variables are coupled and moving, value doesn't get out-competed, it gets relocated. It sharpens [[self-disruption-follows-the-value-chain-downward]] — the value chain doesn't only flow toward the model, it can migrate to the data platform or the device — and it is why [[scaffolding-is-tech-debt-against-the-next-model]] and [[model-compensations-become-liabilities-as-capabilities-advance]]: a capability gain migrates your function into the model. The defense is to own the layer that migration flows *toward*, which is the logic of [[llms-selectively-destroy-vertical-software-moats]].

Great conversation with Jason Graefe of Microsoft about what it actually takes to get AI working inside enterprises.

A few things stood out to me.

The first sale is a trust sale.
Early AI deployments are not judged purely on capability. They are judged on reliability, control, and whether the system can be trusted in real workflows. Founders often underestimate how much of the sale is about de-risking, not just performance.

The model is not the product.
We are moving quickly to a world where model capability is a given. The differentiation is shifting to how well you integrate into workflows, how much context you capture, and how reliably you deliver outcomes.

India has a unique advantage
The depth of engineering talent combined with proximity to global customers and use-cases creates an opportunity to build the "application layer" of AI extremely well, especially in areas that require customization, iteration, and speed.

We're in the first innings of AI.
We are still early in how AI gets embedded into enterprise systems. But the companies that figure out distribution, trust, and workflow integration now will define categories for the next decade.

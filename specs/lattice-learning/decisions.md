# Lattice Learning: Decisions Log

## Decision 1: Framework over one-off session
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Decision:** Create a reusable spec rather than a single-use plan
**Why:** The user recognized that "digesting consumed content into a latticework" is a recurring need, not a one-time event. Making it a spec means it can be applied to future domains, post-book-extraction, post-batch-ingest, etc.
**Alternative rejected:** One-off plan in `.claude/plans/` — would have been lost after the session.

## Decision 2: 5 Big Ideas, not 3 or 7
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Decision:** Cluster into exactly 5 meta-themes for the AI domain
**Why:** 3 is too coarse (would force combining "agents" and "business shifts"). 7 would risk overwhelm. 5 fits the 4-7 range from Principle 4 and a ~60 minute session.

## Decision 3: Interactive conversation over written guide
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Decision:** User chose interactive conversation format for the learning session
**Why:** Dan Koe's article frames processing as active engagement. A written guide would add to the input pile.

## Decision 4: Analogy-first pedagogy
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Decision:** Every term gets its everyday equivalent BEFORE the technical name
**Why:** Non-technical learner. Technical terms without intuitive grounding become meaningless labels.
**Source:** Directly inspired by `latticework-of-models-beats-isolated-facts`.

## Decision 5: Blog post — identity-first, Substack + X article
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Decision:** Write a 2,000-2,500 word blog post, identity-first framing, publishable on Substack and as X long-form article
**Why:** The AI explainer space is crowded with reach-optimized content. What's rare is a non-technical person sharing genuine confusion → clarity with Munger-level thinking. Identity > reach > depth as priority ordering.
**Alternatives rejected:** X thread (user not interested), shorter punchy piece (doesn't do justice to the 5 Big Ideas), longer 3000+ word deep-dive (risks recreating the overwhelm).

## Decision 6: Knowledge graph is part of the story
**Date:** 2026-03-28

**Why:** More authentic than standalone. Shows the real journey. Links to the live graph site for curious readers.

## Decision 7: Anti-slop writing rules
**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Decision:** Explicit rules against AI-sounding writing — no generic openings, no hedging, specific personal details, first person throughout
**Why:** User explicitly stated authenticity is what connects with readers. Koe's article is the style benchmark.

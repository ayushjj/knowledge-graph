# Decision-Making

Frameworks, biases, and structural approaches to making better decisions under uncertainty.

## Key Themes

### Checklists Beat Intuition
Using a systematic list of psychological tendencies as a diagnostic checklist catches compound failures that intuition misses entirely.

### Prevention Over Correction
Once a bad decision locks in (via Inconsistency-Avoidance, social proof, or sunk cost), correction is extremely expensive. Structural prevention at the decision point is far cheaper.

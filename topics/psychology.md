# Psychology

Human psychological tendencies, cognitive biases, and the mechanisms that drive irrational behavior at individual and group levels.

## Key Themes

### Bias Is Structural, Not Moral
Psychological tendencies are evolutionary adaptations that work well on average but produce predictable failures in specific contexts. The antidote is systems design, not willpower.

### Compound Effects Dominate
Individual tendencies are manageable; when multiple tendencies push the same direction (lollapalooza effects), the result is explosive and resistant to correction.

### Self-Assessment Is Systematically Biased
People overrate their own possessions, decisions, and performance. This isn't occasional — it's universal and quantifiable (90% of drivers rate themselves above average).
